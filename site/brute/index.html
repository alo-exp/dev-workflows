Software Stack

How systemic inefficiency across every layer of modern software infrastructure undermines digital sustainability — and what we need to do about it.

CODES / Global Digital Compact
02

"The GDC made sustainability a principle.
Implementation makes it real."

— CODES Secretariat, Townhall Invitation, April 2026

But implementation happens at the infrastructure layer — inside Kubernetes clusters, CI/CD pipelines, and cloud dashboards. That layer has almost no connection to GDC sustainability commitments. The people who control it have no sustainability metric, no mandate, and no tooling to act on what the Compact requires.

This talk presents evidence for a hypothesis: the mainstream software stack is so fundamentally resource-inefficient at every layer that a typical workload consumes 10–30× more energy than an optimised equivalent — and this compounds the digital sector's sustainability crisis at planetary scale.

12 Concrete asks for this townhall

Making sustainability-by-default a runtime behaviour, not a design aspiration

01 Include platform engineers in CODES working groups. DevOps and platform practitioners control the infrastructure layer where sustainability commitments live or die. They are currently absent from GDC implementation bodies.
02 Mandate sustainability metrics in CI/CD tooling. A "green build" status — surfacing carbon and energy impact at the point of infrastructure decisions — should be part of GDC implementation guidance, not an optional add-on.
03 Apply lifecycle sustainability to Global South infrastructure first. Digital infrastructure for developing countries must be built on efficient foundations from the start — not inherit the West's technical debt at scale.
04 Reference: Green Software Foundation's SCI metric, Carbon-Aware SDK, CODES digital sustainability frameworks — already-existing tools that operationalise the GDC's Objective 1(e) commitment at the platform layer.
